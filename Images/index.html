Richard H. Taylor

Scientist-Engineer, Educator, Entrepreneur, Investor, Artist & Poet

Salt Lake City, Utah  ·  richard.taylor@duke.edu  ·  Google Scholar  ·  LinkedIn  ·  Full CV (PDF)

A materials scientist, artist, and poet in Salt Lake City. My work follows one thread — structure — through both materials and meaning: from crystal lattices and high-throughput materials discovery to poetry, sound, and visual art.

Current Work

  • Periodic Graph Metamaterials Atlas (Aux-Mat) — an atlas of the mechanical and dynamical behavior of textile metamaterials, lattices drawn from lace and weave. aux-mat.org
  • A Structure of Harmonic Relations — a theory of harmony as movement through the Stern–Brocot tree of all rationals, with the interactive Consonance Tree as its companion. r-h-taylor.github.io/consonance-tree
  • Materials Engineering: A Narrative Pedagogy — a scholarly edition of Thomas W. Eagar’s recorded MIT teaching (1949–2022): 215 lectures and 1,949 cases, full-text searchable. r-h-taylor.github.io

Artistic Interests

  • Materiality — the substance of a work as its first argument; meaning that lands before anything is read.
  • The symbolic in music — harmony and structure as carriers of conceptual meaning, where consonance and its absence stand in for connection and its loss.
  • The tactile — touch and the made surface; the evidence of the hand.
  • Color — as concept and symbol rather than description.

Background

Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering and Materials Science from Duke. A foundational contributor to the AFLOW framework for high-throughput materials discovery — co-author of its two most-cited papers, with roughly 3,800 citations across the field — with teaching at MIT and research at NIST on the Materials Genome Initiative. A Pushcart-nominated poet and visual artist exhibited across Utah, including the Springville Museum of Art and the public installation KeyChanges at Abravanel Hall Plaza. CEO of Oaki and co-CEO of Woven Pear; co-founder of TP Investments. Vice-Chair of the Peterson School of Music and a board member of the Salt Lake City Arts Council.
